How AC Works

General vapor-compression cycle: compressor, condenser, liquid line, metering device, evaporator, suction line.

What it does

Moves heat from inside the building to outside using refrigerant changing pressure and state.

How to test

  • Check airflow
  • Check temp split
  • Read pressures
  • Compare superheat/subcooling

Common failures

  • Low charge
  • Dirty coils
  • Weak compressor
  • Restriction

Fix direction

Start with airflow and coil condition, then electrical and refrigerant diagnostics. Never add refrigerant without confirming cause.

Wiring Basics

Low-voltage controls tell equipment what to do. High voltage powers motors, compressors, heaters, and controls.

What it does

Connects thermostat calls, safeties, boards, contactors, motors, and compressor circuits.

How to test

  • Power off before touching
  • Check 24V R to C
  • Check Y/G/W/O calls
  • Check line voltage

Common failures

  • Broken wire
  • Blown fuse
  • Loose terminal
  • Failed transformer

Fix direction

Follow diagram, isolate low voltage from line voltage, repair loose/burned wiring, and confirm safeties before replacing boards.

High voltage can kill. Training only.

Compressor Anatomy

The compressor pulls in low-pressure vapor and discharges hot high-pressure vapor.

What it does

Compresses low-pressure refrigerant vapor into hot high-pressure vapor.

How to test

  • Voltage under load
  • Amps vs RLA/LRA
  • Ohm C-S, C-R, S-R
  • Check windings to ground

Common failures

  • Open winding
  • Short to ground
  • Locked rotor
  • Weak capacitor

Fix direction

Confirm power, capacitor, contactor, wiring, and refrigerant conditions before condemning the compressor.

Mini-Split Systems

Outdoor inverter unit and indoor head communicate through wiring and refrigerant lines.

What it does

Provides zoned heating/cooling without traditional ductwork.

How to test

  • Read error codes
  • Check communication voltage
  • Verify line set/vacuum
  • Check sensors

Common failures

  • Communication fault
  • Drain clog
  • Low charge
  • Dirty wheel

Fix direction

Use model code chart, verify wiring polarity/terminals, drain, coil, filter, and sensor readings.

Condenser Coil

Rejects heat outdoors and changes hot vapor into high-pressure liquid.

What it does

Rejects indoor heat and condenses refrigerant.

How to test

  • Inspect cleanliness
  • Check fan
  • Check head pressure
  • Check subcooling

Common failures

  • Dirty coil
  • Bad fan motor
  • Blocked airflow
  • Overcharge

Fix direction

Clean coil, verify fan/motor/capacitor, remove obstruction, then verify refrigerant performance.

Evaporator Coil

Absorbs indoor heat. Refrigerant boils into vapor.

What it does

Absorbs heat from return air and sends cooler air back inside.

How to test

  • Check filter/airflow
  • Check temp split
  • Inspect freeze/dirt
  • Check superheat

Common failures

  • Dirty coil
  • Low airflow
  • Frozen coil
  • Drain clog

Fix direction

Correct airflow first: filter, blower, wheel, coil, ducts. Then evaluate charge/metering.

TXV / Metering Device

Drops pressure and meters refrigerant into the evaporator.

What it does

Drops pressure and controls refrigerant feeding the evaporator.

How to test

  • Check superheat
  • Check bulb placement
  • Check restrictions
  • Use PT chart

Common failures

  • Restricted TXV
  • Bad bulb contact
  • Failed power head
  • Moisture/debris

Fix direction

Verify airflow/charge first, inspect bulb and insulation, check for restriction, then replace only after proving TXV fault.
