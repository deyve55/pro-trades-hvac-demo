How AC Works

Full-screen student lesson: compressor, condenser, liquid line, TXV, evaporator, suction line, vapor, liquid bubbles, and airflow.

Hot High-Pressure Vapor
High-Pressure Liquid
Low-Pressure Liquid/Vapor Mix
Low-Pressure Vapor
Simple explanation: The compressor squeezes low-pressure vapor into hot high-pressure vapor. The condenser rejects heat outside and turns that vapor into high-pressure liquid. The TXV drops pressure and creates a cold liquid/vapor mix. The evaporator absorbs indoor heat, boiling refrigerant back into vapor. The suction line returns vapor to the compressor and the loop repeats.
This is the first active learning lesson. It is visual and animated only. No calculator or diagnosis is active here yet.
